Entities

HTML entities are special codes that represent characters in HTML. They are used to display reserved characters, special symbols, and characters that are not easily typed on a keyboard. Entities start with an ampersand (&) and end with a semicolon (;).

  • &nbsp;: Non-breaking space
  • &lt;: Less than (<)
  • &gt;: Greater than (>)
  • &amp;: Ampersand (&)
  • &quot;: Double quotation mark (")
  • &apos;: Single quotation mark (')
  • &copy;: Copyright symbol (©)
  • &reg;: Registered trademark symbol (®)
  • &trade;: Trademark symbol (™)
  • &euro;: Euro currency symbol (€)
  • &pound;: Pound currency symbol (£)
  • &yen;: Yen currency symbol (¥)
  • &cent;: Cent currency symbol (¢)
  • &sect;: Section symbol (§)
  • &para;: Pilcrow symbol (¶)
  • &bull;: Bullet point (•)
  • &mdash;: Em dash (—)
  • &ndash;: En dash (–)
  • &hellip;: Ellipsis (…)
